The Money Guy 20/3/8 Rule: Your Roadmap to a Smarter Car Purchase

Buying a car can feel a bit like stepping onto a dealership lot in flip-flops. Suddenly you're overwhelmed, underprepared, and at risk of spending far more than you planned. The best option is always to buy what you can afford in cash, freeing up more money for important priorities like paying down high-interest debt. But if you need a car beyond what is currently available to you in cash, taking on a reasonable car loan can make sense, especially if you follow the Money Guy Show's 20/3/8 Rule. Think of it as your financial GPS for navigating a smart, stress-free car purchase.

Step 1: The 20% Down Payment

Start with a solid foundation: put down at least 20% of the car's purchase price. Why? Because it prevents you from being "upside-down" on your loan (owing more than the car is worth, as a typical car depreciates by 10-20% in the first year). Plus, it immediately lowers your monthly payment. Consider it the equivalent of putting on good shoes before you hit that dealership pavement—sturdy, reliable, and confidence-boosting.

Step 2: Finance for 3 Years or Less

This is where discipline pays off. Keep your loan term at three years (36 months) or shorter. It may mean choosing a slightly less flashy ride, but it also means you'll build equity faster and pay less in interest. Shorter terms keep you from dragging a car loan around like unwanted luggage on a cross-country trip.

Step 3: 8% of Your Income

Finally, your car payment should never exceed 8% of your gross monthly income. If you're making $4,000 a month, that means your car payment should be $320 or less. This ensures your shiny new wheels don't wreck your budget. Remember, on your road to financial security, a car is a tool, not a toy.

Putting It All Together

So, when you're eyeing that sleek new sedan (or maybe that SUV that makes you feel like a road warrior), run the 20/3/8 check first. If the numbers don't add up, don't sweat it—there's always another car that will fit both your lifestyle and your wallet.

Following the 20/3/8 Rule may not land you the most jaw-dropping car on the lot, but it will help you drive away with peace of mind, financial freedom, and maybe even enough leftover cash to spring for those heated seats.

Because at the end of the day, the real luxury isn't leather trim; it's knowing you're in the driver's seat of your finances.

How to Budget: Proven Strategies from Trusted Financial Experts

Budgeting is not about restriction; it is about creating a plan that balances financial responsibility with enjoying life. The Money Guys, Brian Preston and Bo Hanson, emphasize that a good budget should be sustainable, flexible, and aligned with your long-term goals. Their approach focuses on percentages, priorities, and intentional spending rather than micromanaging every dollar.

Focus on the Big Three Expenses

The Money Guys often highlight that your financial health depends far more on controlling major expenses than cutting small ones. Instead of obsessing over skipping coffee, focus on:

  • Housing: Keep rent or mortgage payments below 25–28 percent of your gross income.
  • Transportation: Drive cars you can afford without heavy financing. Avoid car payments that strain your monthly cash flow.
  • Food: Plan meals and shop intentionally. Restaurants and takeout can easily overwhelm a budget if not managed.

By optimizing these categories, you free up room for savings and lifestyle spending without feeling deprived.

Use the 20/60/20 Budgeting Framework

A simple, balanced budget can be built using the Money Guys' 20/60/20 rule:

  • 20% for saving and investing. This includes retirement contributions, building an emergency fund, or saving toward big goals.
  • 60% for essentials. Cover housing, utilities, groceries, insurance, transportation, and other non-negotiables.
  • 20% for lifestyle spending. This category funds travel, entertainment, hobbies, dining out, and other discretionary expenses.

This structure ensures you are prioritizing the future while still enjoying the present.

Automate Your Savings and Essentials

The Money Guys stress that budgeting works best when it is automated. Setting up direct deposits or automatic transfers ensures that savings and fixed expenses are handled before you even think about discretionary spending. This reduces the temptation to overspend and helps you stay consistent.

Build in Flexibility

Budgets should adapt as life changes. Review your budget each month and adjust based on new expenses, raises, or unexpected events. The Money Guys recommend planning for irregular expenses with sinking funds—separate savings for items like vacations, car repairs, or holiday gifts. By planning ahead, you avoid debt or budget stress when these costs arrive.

Keep Lifestyle Spending Reasonable

The Money Guys are clear that you do not need to eliminate fun to be financially successful. Instead, cap lifestyle expenses around 20 percent of your income. This gives you freedom to enjoy your money while keeping priorities like savings and essentials front and center.
